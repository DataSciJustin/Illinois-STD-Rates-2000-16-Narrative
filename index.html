Monthly Precipitation in Champaign, IL (1890-2020)

The dataset explored is the accumulated precipitation in Champaign, as measured by the Midwest Regional Climate Center measurement site hosted by the Illinois State Water Survey every month for the years 1890 to 2020. It is updated monthly by survey scientists in collaboration with the MRCC. The Water Survey takes measurements of all precipitation received daily and uploads that information to the MRCC site monthly for public consumption. Of note, that while the precipitation has remained fairly constant, we are entering into a light drought phase and annual snowfall has been steadily decreasing in the past decade.

Data Visualizations

The bar plot below shows the monthly accumulated precipitation, and whether or not it was snowfall, which measures differently than rainfall. The data is set to be be viewed from 1950-2010 by default, but the range bar allows you to set an upper and lower bound to view as much or as little of that data as desired. It also allows for viewing how much of that accumulation was snowfall.
